Basketball

The History of Basketball

In 1891, the game of basketball was invented by a Canadian physical education instructor James Naismith. At that time, James Naismith was given a task to teach a new indoor sport at the international YMCA training school in Springfield, Massachusetts. The creative instructor came up with the idea to create an indoor game played during the cold winters of Massachusetts that relied on skills, the game is what we now call basketball. James Naismith came up with 13 simple rules and used a soccer ball and two peach baskets for the setup of the first game. In the 20th century, the game grew very popular nationwide and equipments were modified for the convenience of the game . In 1936 it became an official Olympic sport played during the Berlin Olympics. After the quick establishment, professional games followed. The most well known basketball association - The American National Basketball Association (NBA) established in 1946. Basketball grew to a multibillion-dollar enterprise by the end of the century.

Objective of Basketball

The game is played with 2 teams. Each team consists of 5 players on the court at a time. The object of the game is to outscore your opponent by shooting the ball into your basket and preventing them from putting the ball into theirs. The ball can be advanced up the floor with the hands only, either by dribbling or passing to teammates.

Equipment and court requirements for basketball

  • Wood or maple floor flat and rectangular in shape
  • A basketball
  • Metal rimed Basket/Hoop
  • Scoreboard
  • Backboard attatched with the basket
  • Team uniforms

Volleyball

The history of volleyball

The game of volleyball, originally called “mintonette,” was invented in 1895 by on William G. Morgan after the invention of basketball only four years before. Morgan, a graduate of the Springfield College of the YMCA, designed the game to be a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball.

The object of volleyball

The object of the game volleyball is for each team to send the ball regularly over the net to ground it on the opponent's court, and to prevent the ball from being grounded on its own court. The ball is put into play by the right back-row player who serves the ball by hitting it over the net to the opponent's court. A team is allowed to hit the ball three times (in addition to the block contact) to return it to the opponent's court. A player is not allowed to hit the ball twice consecutively, except when attempting a block. The rally continues until the ball touches the ground/floor, goes "out" or a team fails to return it to the opponent's court or commits a fault.

Equipment and court requirements for volleyball

  • An indoor court measured 18 meters long by 9 meters wide
  • A volleyball
  • Volleyball net
  • Kneepads
  • Elbow pads
